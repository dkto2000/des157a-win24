Thoughts on Form Design

January 23rd, 2024

I recently read the article titled Best Practices for Form Design on Medium, and it highlighted several insightful points about effective form design. Let's face it, nobody likes filling out forms! Most if not all of the fill-in portions can be executed through a click or two. The article introduced new concepts to me. For instance, I discovered the importance of limiting Inline label design because users can be disoriented once the label clears upon clicking it. I also found out the importance of careful wording when it comes to giving the user feedback once they have messed up. Inputting a negative message such as "You didn't enter a name" puts unnecessary blame on them.

I find the Twitter Sign Up Page to be a good example of form design because of how simplified the process is. There is a great balance of clicks and fill-ins and the warnings carry a neutral connotation.
